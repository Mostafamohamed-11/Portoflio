About Me



“Every great product tells a story. My role? To make sure the story is never interrupted by bugs or glitches.”

As a Computer Science student at the Arab Academy for Science, Technology, and Maritime Transport, I am driven by a strong passion for technology, problem-solving, and software development. I specialize in ensuring that software is not just built, but built right. I thrive at the intersection of curiosity and precision — where I uncover hidden flaws, improve performance, and guarantee user satisfaction.

I am also part of the prestigious Digital Egypt Pioneers Initiative (DEPI), a national scholarship from the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT). Within the Software Testing track, I specialize in ensuring that software is not only functional but also reliable, user-friendly, and high in quality.

I believe that software quality is not just a requirement—it’s a responsibility. Every line of code should deliver value, and every application should make users’ lives easier.

When I’m not running test cases, you’ll find me exploring new tools, pushing the limits of automation, and constantly leveling up my skills. For me, testing isn’t just a job — it’s craftsmanship.”
